<p><p>The agricultural animal vaccine market comprises primarily three types: live attenuated vaccines, inactivated vaccines, and others. Live attenuated vaccines use weakened pathogens to stimulate an immune response, offering robust protection with a single dose. Inactivated vaccines contain killed pathogens, providing safety but often requiring multiple doses for effective immunity. The "others" category includes subunit, recombinant, and DNA vaccines, which utilize specific antigens or genetic material to provoke immune responses. Together, these types enhance animal health and productivity in farming.</p></p>

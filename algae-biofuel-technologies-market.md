<p><p>The algae biofuel technologies market encompasses three primary cultivation systems. Open cultivation systems utilize natural sunlight and water bodies, offering low production costs but facing challenges like contamination and environmental variability. Closed cultivation systems, such as photobioreactors, provide controlled environments, enhancing yield and purity but at higher operational costs. Sea-based cultivation systems harness ocean resources, promoting large-scale biomass production with minimal land use; however, they must contend with marine conditions and logistical complexities for processing and transportation.</p></p>

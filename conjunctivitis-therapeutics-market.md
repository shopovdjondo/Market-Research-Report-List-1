<p><p>The conjunctivitis therapeutics market comprises three primary types: allergic, bacterial, and viral conjunctivitis. Allergic conjunctivitis is often treated with antihistamines and anti-inflammatory agents to alleviate symptoms triggered by allergens. Bacterial conjunctivitis is addressed with antibiotic eye drops or ointments targeting the infectious bacteria. Viral conjunctivitis, typically self-limiting, may require symptomatic relief through cold compresses or lubricating eye drops, as antiviral treatments are limited. Each type reflects distinct therapeutic approaches tailored to specific underlying causes, influencing market dynamics.</p></p>

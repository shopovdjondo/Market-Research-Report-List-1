<p><p>The coreless motor market comprises two primary types: brushed and brushless coreless motors. Brushed coreless motors utilize carbon brushes for commutation, offering simplicity and cost-effectiveness, making them suitable for low-power applications. In contrast, brushless coreless motors employ electronic commutation, eliminating brush wear and enhancing efficiency and lifespan, ideal for high-performance applications. Both types cater to diverse sectors such as robotics, drones, and miniaturized devices, driven by their compact design, lightweight characteristics, and superior performance capabilities.</p></p>

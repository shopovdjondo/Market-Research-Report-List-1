<p><p>The malignant glioma drugs market comprises various treatment categories including alkylating agents, VEGF/VEGFR inhibitors, and anti-angiogenic drugs. Alkylating agents work by damaging DNA to inhibit cancer cell replication. VEGF/VEGFR inhibitors target the vascular endothelial growth factor pathways to block tumor blood supply, hindering growth. Anti-angiogenic drugs focus on preventing new blood vessel formation, thus limiting tumor progression and improving patient survival. Together, these therapies contribute significantly to the management of malignant gliomas, enhancing treatment outcomes.</p></p>

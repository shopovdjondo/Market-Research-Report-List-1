<p><p>Positive electrode materials for lithium batteries play a crucial role in determining performance, energy density, and lifespan. Lithium Cobalt Oxide (LCO) is known for its high energy density but limited thermal stability. Nickel-Cobalt-Manganese (NCM) combines stability and capacity, making it popular in electric vehicle applications. Lithium Manganese Oxide (LMO) offers excellent thermal stability but lower capacity. Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) is favored for safety and longevity, while Nickel Cobalt Aluminum (NCA) achieves high performance, especially in automotive uses. Each material has unique advantages suited for different applications.</p></p>
